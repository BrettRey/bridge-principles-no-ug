# Revision Plan From Reviews
## Keep Fixed
- Preserve the `\enquote{linguists}` / `\term{linguists}` distinction as it stands.
  
- Keep Oxford spelling.
  
- Keep the paper's main thesis: crosslinguistic inference requires bridge principles, not a UG monopoly.
  
## Core Revisions
1. Strengthen the introduction and monopoly section by making the two-horn reply explicit:
  
  - if UG merely makes evidence potentially relevant, the monopoly conclusion does not follow;
    
  - if a substantive UG theory licenses particular transfers, then it is one bridge under audit, not a privileged escape from bridge-principle discipline.
    
2. Add a direct answer to Reiss's domain-individuation move:
  
  - human languages are grouped not by a bare UG essence but by projectible profiles involving human learning, externalization, interpretation, communicative uptake, diachronic transmission, and social maintenance;
    
  - this includes Warlpiri and French while excluding chess, Python, and tango because those do not support the same cluster of linguistic projections.
    
3. Add one successful non-UG evidential-transfer case:
  
  - historical-comparative reconstruction is the best compact case;
    
  - its warrant is common descent, regular correspondence, chronology, borrowing/contact diagnostics, and defeaters;
    
  - evidence from one language can bear on another without UG because the bridge is causal-historical.
    
4. Slow the projectibility machinery slightly:
  
  - add a brief schematic example after cue/scoped/robust support grades;
    
  - make `target`, `profile`, `diagnostic`, `stabilizer`, `scope`, and `defeater` legible without requiring the reader to know the KAPP manuscript.
    
5. Sharpen the French `any` audit:
  
  - bring in Hoeksema as support for the claim that NPI classifications are distributional and item-sensitive, not label-driven;
    
  - note cautiously that French `rien` is not a clean realization of English NPI `any` because it is entangled with negative-quantifier / n-word behaviour;
    
  - use this to skewer the classification step, not to overclaim.
    
6. Retarget the public-language section:
  
  - frame it as answering Reiss's individuation worry rather than accusing him of denying all public-language inquiry;
    
  - keep Standard written English as a projectible public target, but make one concrete projection explicit.
