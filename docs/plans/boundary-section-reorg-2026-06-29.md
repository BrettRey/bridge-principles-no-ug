# Boundary Section Reorganization
Date: 2026-06-29
## Diagnosis
The current fix treats the problem as missing quotation context inside Section 4. That is only partly right. The structural problem is that the boundary-stipulation material is not a separate stage of the paper's argument. It is a scope condition on the monopoly claim.

Section 3 argues that Reiss moves from a domain postulate to a transfer monopoly without supplying the missing transfer and exclusion warrants. The `linguists`/`\term{linguists}` distinction is another instance of that same overreach: a local stipulation about Chomskyan-internal theorists is made to bear on \term{linguists} generally.
## Proposed Fix
1. Keep the introduction's typography note, but do not send the reader forward to a later section for the textual basis.

2. Fold the current "Boundary Stipulation" material into the end of "The Monopoly Claim."

3. Put the motivating Reiss quote immediately where the boundary point is made:

  - \enquote{the views of `linguists' in this chapter}

  - \enquote{Chomsky and those who broadly adopt his philosophical stances}

  - \enquote{self-identify as linguists}

4. Remove the standalone "Boundary Stipulation" section heading.

5. Let "The Projectibility Framework" follow directly after the monopoly section. That makes the structure:

  - what Reiss gets right;

  - why the monopoly inference fails;

  - how projectibility supplies the alternative.

6. Update the roadmap so it no longer advertises a freestanding Section 4 for typography.

## Intended Effect
The paper stops asking a short terminological point to carry a full section. The `linguists` issue becomes what it really is: a local scope warning inside the monopoly argument. The quotes stay adjacent to the inference they motivate, and the projectibility framework arrives sooner as the positive alternative.
## Guardrails
- Do not make the point sound like a personal complaint about Reiss.

- Preserve the concession that Reiss may stipulate locally.

- Preserve the stronger objection: local stipulation does not license field-wide conclusions.

- Do not add citations or examples.
