# Project Brief: Bridge Principles without UG

## Aim

Write a short paper responding to Reiss's claim that the postulate of Universal Grammar licenses crosslinguistic evidence. The useful wedge is that Reiss identifies a real problem: crosslinguistic evidence needs bridge principles. The disagreement is that the existence of that problem does not establish Chomskyan Universal Grammar as the only possible license.

## Working Thesis

Crosslinguistic inference requires bridge principles; it does not require Chomskyan Universal Grammar. UG is one proposed license for evidential travel. Others are available when projection targets, explanatory levels, stabilizers, and failure conditions are made explicit.

## Productive Concessions

First, theoretical linguistics can be empirical without being experimental, corpus-based, or statistical.

Second, categories such as word, sentence, negative-polarity item, segment, syllable, and wh-element are not read directly from raw speech, text, or corpora.

Third, crosslinguistic comparison is not automatic. French facts can help analyse English `any`, but only if there is an account of why the distinction travels.

These concessions should sharpen the disagreement. The paper is not denying theory, abstraction, realism, or comparison. It denies the monopoly claim.

## Main Objection

Reiss moves among several claims:

1. Human languages are comparable.
2. Individual mental grammars are real objects.
3. The relevant primitives are innate and genetically specified.
4. Crosslinguistic evidence can bear on the analysis of particular languages.
5. Only UG licenses that evidential travel.
6. Non-UG linguists lack grounds for importing categories across languages.

The vulnerable step is the move from bridge principles to UG as the only possible bridge.

## Boundary-Policing Point

The phrase `self-identified linguists` should not be the centre of the response, but it matters as evidence of boundary policing. The more important passage is the chapter's stipulation that, unless otherwise indicated, `linguists` means Chomsky and those who broadly share his philosophical stance. That restriction may be harmless for reconstructing Chomskyan methodology. It is not harmless if the chapter then draws conclusions about what linguists as such can do.

## Rhetorical Stance

Follow Rapaport's rules: reconstruct the target argument charitably, concede the strongest defensible points, quote accurately, and avoid cheap shots. After that, press the critique hard. The paper should expose the monopoly claim and the boundary-policing move as forcefully as the evidence allows.

## Positive Alternative

A projectibility account asks:

1. What is the projection target?
2. What observable profile supports it?
3. What relations make the profile non-accidental?
4. What stabilizes the profile across contexts or timescales?
5. What failure modes mark its limits?
6. For which purposes, fields, or practices is the profile projectible?

Possible bridge principles include shared projection targets, stabilizing mechanisms, acquisition and transmission bottlenecks, diachronic pathways, recurrent communicative pressures, population-level conventions, typological recurrence, and independent linkage evidence.

## Public and Distributed Objects

Public, community-level, and normatively regimented objects need not be compact individual substances to be scientifically tractable. They can be maintained, taken up, corrected, transmitted, institutionalized, and projectible. The reply to the Standard English/on-the-moon joke is that distributed norm-governed kinds are being misindividuated as medium-sized objects.

## Drafting Constraints

- Keep the paper a response with a positive alternative, not a general literature review.
- Do not make the rhetorical boundary-policing point do more work than the methodological argument.
- Avoid adding citations until keys and bibliographic details are verified against authoritative sources.
- Run a projectibility audit after the first serious draft.
- Run level/category error and terminological hygiene checks before preprint or journal submission.
