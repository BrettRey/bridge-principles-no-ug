# Review Board Synthesis

## Consensus Strengths

- The concession-first structure works. Reviewers agreed that granting Reiss armchair method, theory-mediation, and the bridge-principle burden makes the disagreement sharper.
- The reflexive move on Reiss's stipulated `\enquote{linguists}` is promising, but only if the draft quotes the passage where the local stipulation does global exclusionary work.
- The projectibility schema is the right machinery: projection target, profile, diagnostics, ordering relation, stabilizer, scope, and defeaters.

## Consensus Weaknesses

- The draft asserts Reiss's monopoly claim without quoting it. This is the most urgent source-grounding fix.
- The draft characterizes UG as a bare postulate too strongly. A formalist will object that substantive UG supplies concrete crosslinguistic hypotheses and defeaters.
- The projectibility alternative is stated but not instantiated. The draft needs one worked crosslinguistic inference that succeeds or fails under the schema.
- The public-language section asserts tractability by analogy. It needs one compact worked public/distributed target with projection, diagnostics, stabilizers, scope, and defeaters.
- Haspelmath/Croft are currently named as alternatives, not shown to license evidential travel. The draft needs to distinguish comparison, classification, and transfer.

## Contradictions and Tensions

- The Chomskyan formalist says projectibility should be framed as a meta-framework that contains UG as one case; the hostile methodologist wants a case where projectibility blocks what UG permits. The revision should say: projectibility is the audit discipline, not a rival language faculty, but it exposes why UG cannot be the monopoly bridge for all linguistic targets.
- The public-language reviewer wants more ontology and philosophy-of-language literature; the methodologist wants less analogy and more template application. The revision should prioritize the template application and cite Stainton/Pullum-Scholz enough to ground the point without expanding into a survey.
- The typology reviewer warns that Haspelmath's comparative concepts may support commensurable description rather than evidential transfer. The revision should concede that distinction and treat it as a feature: comparative concepts license some comparisons and block others.

## Prioritized Revision List

1. Quote Reiss's UG exclusivity and `\enquote{linguists}` stipulation directly, including the final \enquote{anyone who denies UG} claim.
2. Reframe the UG objection: not that substantive UG never specifies projections, but that Reiss's monopoly conclusion does not follow from UG's successes within I-language inquiry.
3. Add a worked crosslinguistic case using NPI/anaphor-style evidence: specify target, source features, profile, diagnostics, ordering relation, stabilizers, scope, and defeaters; show how Haspelmath-style comparison localizes transfer rather than permitting category import.
4. Add a compact public-standard/variety worked case using conditioning structure and demotion conditions.
5. Tighten profile vocabulary: distinguish worldly profile from epistemic profile specification; add minimal support grades.
6. Fix acknowledgement placeholder and run full checks.
