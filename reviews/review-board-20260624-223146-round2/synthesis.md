# Second Review Board Synthesis

## Consensus Strengths

- The revision fixed the original abstraction problem: reviewers agreed that the paper now states a concrete target/profile/diagnostics/scope structure and no longer reads as a pure framework sketch.
- The concession-first structure still works. All reviewers saw the paper as anti-monopoly rather than anti-UG.
- The support-grade scale and demotion requirement are the strongest positive contribution.

## Consensus Weaknesses

- The French-English `any` case should not be presented as a full non-UG transfer unless the ordering relation and stabilizer are actually filled.
- French evidence is cue-level on the paper's own taxonomy. The argument should exploit that fact against Reiss rather than hide it.
- The paper needs to distinguish projectibility as a second-order audit from first-order bridge fillings such as UG, functional-typological recurrence, diachronic pathway, or institutional maintenance.
- The public-language section should be framed as the ontology half of Reiss's restriction, not as a second crosslinguistic transfer case.

## Revision Decisions

1. Recast the `any` section as a worked audit rather than a worked bridge.
2. State that projectibility demotes French evidence to cue level unless target, diagnostics, ordering relation, stabilizer, scope, and defeaters are supplied.
3. Replace parsimony as the ordering relation with a compositional/distributional dependency claim.
4. Treat candidate stabilizers as hypotheses that require defence, not as the evidence itself.
5. Add the comparison/classification/evidential-transfer triad.
6. Clarify that public standards are social-practical targets and that Section 7 answers Reiss's object-restriction move.

## Residual Risk

The paper now demolishes Reiss's monopoly inference more than it supplies a fully source-grounded independent case of crosslinguistic transfer. That is acceptable for this draft's aim if the introduction and conclusion keep the thesis as "bridge principles without UG monopoly" rather than "one complete non-UG bridge for the `any` case."
