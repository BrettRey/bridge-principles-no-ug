## Projectibility Review: *Bridge Principles without UG*

### 1. Main claim (one sentence)
Crosslinguistic inference does require a bridge principle to license evidential travel, but Chomskyan UG is only one candidate licence, and a projectibility account (target, profile, diagnostics, ordering relation, stabilizers, scope, defeaters) supplies an accountable, pluralist alternative.

### 2. Strengths

- **Projectibility is the spine, not a coat of paint.** The same seven-slot template is defined in §5 (line 75), applied to two cases (§6 *any*, §7 Standard Written English / varieties), and restated as the operative demand in the conclusion (line 133). The contribution is cashed out inferentially in the abstract, intro, and conclusion ("what a proposed category lets us infer"), never as "here are the mechanisms." On the check-hpc framing test, this draft passes cleanly, which is rare.

- **The HPC/homeostasis deprecation is done correctly, not just lexically.** "Property cluster + homeostatic mechanisms" has been replaced by "profile + ordering + stabilizers," and stabilizers are explicitly subordinated to projection and denied magic-pass status: "None of those is a magic pass. Each has to explain why observed source properties and projected target properties are co-available" (lines 91-93). That keeps projection primary and stabilizers instrumental, which is the exact inversion the framework needs.

- **The reflexive turn is the best idea and it's projectibility-driven.** Applying the bridge-principle demand back onto Reiss via the "linguists" (Chomskyan subkind) vs. `\term{linguists}` (projectible category) distinction (§4, lines 65-69) converts a potential polemic into a scope diagnosis: a local stipulation can't do global work without its own bridge. This is charitable, sharp, and entirely a projectibility move.

### 3. Remaining weaknesses (actionable)

- **The flagship example misfills two of its own seven slots, exactly the two that carry warrant.** In §6 the "ordering relation" is stated as a parsimony argument ("a one-form analysis loses parsimony at a deeper level," line 102), but §5 defines the ordering relation as "what makes source and target properties co-available rather than accidentally associated" (line 75), paradigmatically "formal dependency" (line 91). Parsimony is theory choice, not a co-availability relation. Likewise the "stabilizers" (line 103) are listed as *evidential* facts (recurrence across environments, availability of independent English diagnostics, existence of overt splits elsewhere), none of which is a stabilizer in the §5 sense (acquisition pressure, processing bias, diachronic pathway, uptake, institution). So the one example billed as "the best test case" (line 96) under-delivers on warrant. Fix: model §6 on §7, where the ordering relation ("correction, publication, assessment, and uptake partly make the standard," line 123) and stabilizers ("schooling, publishing, examination," line 123) are filled cleanly. A genuine ordering relation for *any* is the compositional-semantic dependence (existential-under-downward-entailment vs. free-choice/modal interpretation); a genuine stabilizer is whatever recurrently splits those functions (a grammaticalization pathway, say), or mark it openly as unspecified rather than filling it with evidence.

- **The support-grade scale is under-exercised and terminologically unstable.** The abstract's triad is "diagnostic cue, a scoped bridge, and a warranted importation of categories" (line 22); §5's grade scale is "diagnostic cue / scoped bridge / robust bridge" (line 79). The third terms differ, and they're on different axes: robustness is a *strength* of a bridge, importation is a *rival model* the paper rejects. Only the scoped-bridge rung is ever climbed, the *any* case is never explicitly graded, and no case contrasts two grades. For the machinery to read as load-bearing rather than announced, align the abstract with §5, keep "importation" out of the grade list, explicitly grade *any* as scoped-not-robust, and show at least one up- or down-grade.

- **The *any* case may quietly undercut the paper's own promise about evidential travel.** French is demoted to "a diagnostic cue ... not because it transfers a French category into English" (line 101), and the real warrant becomes English-internal diagnostics plus parsimony. That's rhetorically strong against Reiss, but it means the middle rung (a scoped bridge that actually carries crosslinguistic projection) is nearly collapsible into monolingual reanalysis prompted by a foreign cue. The "scoped bridge" therefore isn't really exhibited. Consider adding or substituting a case where crosslinguistic evidence licenses an inference that language-internal evidence alone can't, so the scoped-bridge grade does visible work.

Minor residue (the task asked me to flag homeostasis vocabulary): "The projected cluster is sustained engagement..." (line 65) reintroduces the deprecated "cluster"; prefer "profile" or "pattern." And "co-availability" should always be glossed as co-available *for projection*, never left as bare co-occurrence, since bare co-occurrence is the homeostasis idea the paper means to retire.

### 4. Support-grade machinery and the *any* reconstruction, slot by slot

The reconstruction specifies five of seven slots well and two poorly. The two poorly specified slots are the ones that explain *why* the projection holds rather than *that* it holds and *where* it stops.

| Slot | Verdict | Note |
|------|---------|------|
| Target | Clear | "the analysis of English *any*: whether a single surface form realizes one lexical-semantic item or two" (line 99). Correctly localized to the analysis, not "English as such." |
| Profile | Clear | "the recurring contrast between items dependent on licensing environments and items associated with free-choice or modal-like interpretation" (line 99). Correctly de-localized from French. |
| Diagnostics | Strong | DE contexts/negation/questions/conditionals vs. free-choice/modal/indifference readings (line 101); French translation explicitly demoted to a cue. Best-filled slot. |
| Ordering relation | **Misfilled** | Stated as parsimony (line 102), not a co-availability relation as defined in §5 (line 75). |
| Stabilizers | **Misfilled** | Evidential facts listed (line 103), not worldly stabilizers per §5 (line 91). |
| Scope | Clear | "the analysis of the English polarity/free-choice contrast, not the unrestricted importation of French categories" (line 103). |
| Defeaters | Model-grade | Four genuine defeaters plus Reiss's own complications (negative concord, dialect variation, written/spoken mismatch) folded in as defeaters rather than footnotes (line 105). |

Support-grade machinery overall: the idea is right and well-motivated ("These grades aren't decorations. They're how a bridge principle avoids becoming an all-purpose permission slip," line 79), but it is asserted more than demonstrated, and the abstract/§5 misalignment on the third grade has to be repaired before it reads as a working scale.

(I have not cited external scholarship here; my suggestion about the compositional semantics of NPI vs. free-choice *any* is a structural proposal for filling the slot, not a sourced claim being inserted into the paper. If you take it up in the text, the relevant NPI/free-choice literature would need source verification before any specific attribution.)

### 5. What would make this polished rather than promising

- Repair §6 so the flagship case honors §5's own definitions of ordering relation and stabilizer, using §7 as the template. This is the single highest-leverage fix, because §6 is precisely where a hostile reader will claim the vocabulary is decorative.
- Make the support-grade scale discriminate on a real case: align abstract and §5, grade *any* explicitly as a scoped (not robust) bridge, and show one upgrade or downgrade.
- Add a case (or recast *any*) where crosslinguistic evidence genuinely carries projection, so the scoped-bridge rung isn't collapsible into monolingual analysis.
- Add one short paragraph stating what would defeat or embarrass the projectibility account itself. The paper demands defeaters from everyone else (Reiss, comparative concepts, public-language objects); stating its own would close the reflexive loop and pre-empt the obvious "physician, heal thyself" reply.
- Scrub the residual "cluster" (line 65) and tighten "co-availability" to "co-available for projection" throughout.

### 6. Verdict

**Revise & Resubmit (moderate).** Projectibility is genuinely the organizing discipline here, not a decorative layer, so this is well above Reject; but the headline *any* reconstruction misfills two of its own seven slots and the support-grade scale is announced rather than exercised, which is exactly the evidence a skeptic needs to call the machinery ornamental, so it isn't yet an Accept.
