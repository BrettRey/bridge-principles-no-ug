## Cross-model consensus (both boards)

1. **F2 over-concession — fix line 53 (unanimous, 10/10).** The sentence "successful
   UG-shaped warrants for I-language inquiry..." reads as conceding that I-language
   inquiry is UG-warranted. All ten cite Reiss's own §3: "belief in I-language does not
   entail belief in UG-object." The transfer-licensing role attaches to UG-*object*,
   not to I-language inquiry. Refinement (projectibility + typology): "successful" must
   mean a thick, audited UG theory under the same discipline as every other warrant,
   not the bare postulate. Keep target (I-language) and bridge (thick UG theory) apart.

2. **F3 target legitimate, but tighten (unanimous).** Both hostile reviewers went in to
   prove "the monopoly claim is the author's paraphrase" and both reported it FAILS:
   Reiss states exclusivity verbatim three times — "soap bubbles" (§5.3.1), "Only the
   belief in Universal Grammar" (§5.3.3), "Anyone who denies UG has no grounds...
   competing theoretical approaches" (§6). Actions: (a) quote those three at the point
   of attack (currently paraphrased); (b) add the "competing theoretical approaches"
   clause — the single strongest anti-retreat closer, not yet cited; (c) drop the
   psychologizing periphery: "turns colleagues into claimants," and convert "runs
   together"/"tends to run together"/"often lets the first do the work" into a precise
   textual claim. Reiss's §3 carefully separates UG-object/UG-theory/I-language, so the
   conflation to allege is specifically domain/transfer/exclusion, not "his terms."

3. **F1 the big gap — VERTICAL vs HORIZONTAL (near-unanimous, both boards).** The only
   worked non-UG warrant is historical-comparative = vertical (within a family). Reiss's
   exclusivity lives in horizontal transfer between UNRELATED languages (Japanese on
   English; the Rim/Sarah/Kyle/Hisako case). The paper concedes horizontal is "a further
   project" (line 155). Consequence: the literal/unscoped §6 claim is refuted by the
   vertical case (good), but if Reiss retreats to "I meant horizontal I-language transfer
   between unrelated languages," the paper has, by its own admission, no worked
   counterexample — only the French demotion-to-cue plus a promissory note. The negative
   thesis (UG is not the SOLE licence) survives; the positive horizontal thesis is
   undelivered. This is the hostile-Codex Reject ground and every other reviewer's main
   weakness. Two routes offered: (a) deliver ONE worked horizontal non-UG bridge
   (Haspelmath implicational universal / semantic map; functional-typological recurrence
   as support profile; known exceptions as defeaters; answers the mo/ka case on its own
   ground); or (b) make the negative/positive scope explicit and stop implying more
   (reframe title/abstract so the genealogical case is the advertised counterexample and
   horizontal typological transfer is marked unfinished).

4. **Projectibility load-bearing, not decorative (both projectibility reviewers, GREEN).**
   Refinements: front-load one positive projection in §4 so prediction leads and the
   demotion of UG follows (the paper currently reads as veto-first); pull the concrete
   style-guide projection (who/whom, its/it's, between you and I in edited prose) into
   the conclusion, which currently leaves public-standard/typological projections at the
   meta-level. Key challenge: name one NOVEL positive projection a non-UG bridge licenses
   that a UG-monopoly view gets wrong — not a demotion.

5. **Section 8 weakest (public-language + hostile + typology).** (a) It risks reading as
   relabeling a self-maintaining norm rather than answering individuation. NOTE (Brett's
   correction): the fix is PROJECTIBILITY-FIRST, not the reviewer's "HPC / constitutive
   cluster-stabilizer" framing. Individuate the public object by the projections it licenses
   under declared defeaters (testable, so not relabeling); maintenance mechanisms stabilize
   that profile but are not its constitutive essence. Do NOT make maintenance constitutive of
   a kind — that imports mechanism-first HPC and breaks level-discipline. (b) The moon rebuttal beats the literal
   transport test but not the natural-object/concreteness criterion driving Reiss, and
   Reiss BRACKETS public languages ("a different kind of thing... irrelevant to my goals,"
   §2) rather than banning them — reframe §8 as "bracketing is not refuting," quote §2.
   (c) §8 reads as a second paper UNLESS routed through the hinge two reviewers found
   independently: the typological comparanda that license horizontal transfer ARE
   public/distributed/non-mental objects, so the ontological half becomes a precondition
   of completing the evidential half, not a parallel application. This connects #3 and #5.

6. **Haspelmath/Croft cited, not worked (typology, strongest single point).** They appear
   once (line 99) and are dropped where they'd do most work: the French audit (Haspelmath's
   comparative-concept distinction independently yields "demote to cue") and Croft's denial
   of universal categories, a frontal contradiction of Reiss's EAD 2/EAD 7 ("recurrent
   categories are real natural objects"). Work them, don't name-drop. Directly answers
   Brett's "cite Haspelmath and Croft" instruction: the board says cite = mobilize.

7. **Substance-free-features rebuttal (typology, sharp new point).** Reiss will say the
   comparative method presupposes UG-supplied phonological features (Chomsky & Halle 1965
   — the exact move in Reiss's email). Decisive reply: correspondences are stated over
   PHONETIC substance, and Reiss & Volenec defend SUBSTANCE-FREE features, so Reiss
   specifically cannot claim the comparative method needs HIS features. This sharpens the
   feature-vocabulary reply and turns Reiss's own program against the email's point.

evidence"). The board pushes the opposite discipline: the literal universal claim is
refuted, but Reiss retreats to horizontal-I-language, where only the negative result is

validated), but temper the global closers — state the negative thesis precisely, anchor
"condition of possibility" to the specific exclusivity sentences, and either deliver or
explicitly flag the horizontal case.
